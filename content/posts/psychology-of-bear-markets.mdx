# The Fear Index

When the VIX spikes, rational thought often exits the building. In financial markets, pain is the only true teacher, but most investors refuse to learn the lesson.

## The Capitulation Point

Capitulation isn't just a red candle on a chart; it's a visceral emotional state. It's the moment when the pain of holding becomes greater than the fear of missing out on a future recovery.

> "The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient." — Warren Buffett

### Key Mental Models for Downturns

1.  **Inversion**: Instead of asking "How much can I make?", ask "How much can I afford to lose?"
2.  **Time Horizon Arbitrage**: If you can hold for 5 years when others can only hold for 5 days, you have an edge.
3.  **Probabilistic Thinking**: Nothing is certain. Everything is a probability distribution.

## Algorithmic Detachment

The best traders I know don't feel. They execute. Building a system—whether automated or a strict set of rules—is the only way to bypass the lizard brain that screams "SELL" when the screen turns red.
